What is this?

A System for Training the Mind

In 1912, Charles F. Haanel — a successful St. Louis businessman — began distributing a correspondence course unlike anything else of its era. Rather than offering advice or motivation, it offered a system: a precise, progressive training of the mind based on what he called the laws governing mental action.

The course ran for 24 weeks. Each week, students received one lesson to read and one exercise to practice daily. By Part 24, students had moved from simply sitting still, all the way to a working recognition of their connection with universal creative power.

Napoleon Hill studied it and credited it as a primary source for what became Think and Grow Rich. The book has been in continuous publication — in various forms — for over a century.

"With the realization that mind is the great creative power, what does not become possible? With Desire as the great creative energy, can we not see why Desire should be cultivated, controlled and directed in our lives and destinies?"
— Charles F. Haanel, The Master Key System (1919)
How the exercises work

One Practice. One Week. Twenty-Four Weeks.

  1. 1
    One exercise per week Haanel designed the course to be taken one part per week. Each exercise builds on the previous one — rushing ahead skips the foundation. Spend a full week with each practice before moving on.
  2. 2
    Find a quiet place and sit Every exercise is a sitting practice — 15 to 30 minutes in a chair, spine upright, eyes closed. You don't need a meditation cushion or special room. A quiet chair where you won't be disturbed is all that's required.
  3. 3
    Use the timer and the Read Aloud feature Set your preferred duration (10–30 min) and press Begin. Before you close your eyes, tap "Read aloud" — a calm voice will guide you through the full exercise instructions, then fall silent while you practice.
  4. 4
    Practice daily, not just once The original course expected students to practice each exercise every day for seven days. Even one or two sessions a week will produce results — but daily practice is where the real change happens.
  5. 5
    Write in your journal after each session Each exercise page has a notes field. Use it. Writing after a sitting session captures insights that evaporate quickly — and reveals patterns across weeks you wouldn't otherwise notice.
  6. 6
    Mark the session complete, then return when ready When you're done, mark the session complete to log it. Your progress is saved locally in your browser. Haanel recommended staying with each part for a full week before moving on — until the exercise feels effortless. That's the intended pace, though you're free to follow your own rhythm.
The progression

Where the 24 Weeks Take You

The course moves through three broad phases:

Parts 1–4 — Physical and mental control. You learn to sit still, quiet the mind, fully relax the body, and recognize the difference between your body/mind (instruments) and the "I" that directs them.

Parts 5–14 — Mental creation and the laws of mind. You build vivid mental images, concentrate on single ideas, work with affirmations, explore the law of attraction, and begin consciously directing thought as a creative force.

Parts 15–24 — Universal principles and integration. You work with growth, vibration, insight, truth, inspiration, health, and money consciousness — arriving finally at the recognition that the same intelligence animating the cosmos is available to you.

Ready to begin? Part 1 takes 15 minutes. All you need is a quiet chair.
